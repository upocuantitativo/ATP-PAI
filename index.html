About This Project

Research Context

The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in tourism is profoundly transforming travel planning and experience delivery. AI offers significant potential for improving accessibility; however, its uncritical application may generate new dynamics of exclusion. Despite growing interest in both accessible tourism and AI-driven tourism, very few studies integrate both fields in a critical and systematic manner (Korbiel et al., 2025; Wang et al., 2025).

The TAP-IAP Model

The TAP-IAP model (Turismo Accesible Potenciado por Inteligencia Artificial Personalizada / Accessible Tourism Powered by Personalized AI) is a conceptual framework designed to analyze and guide the design of inclusive tourism experiences mediated by intelligent systems. Its objective is to orient the design, governance, and evaluation of tourism technologies from a perspective centered on the rights, autonomy, and dignity of persons with functional diversity.

Purpose of This Tool

This proof-of-concept operationalizes the TAP-IAP model as an interactive decision-support and ex ante evaluation tool, illustrating its practical applicability. It materializes the theoretical framework into functional modules: accessible profiling, explainable algorithmic personalization, bias review, and an indicator dashboard with scenario comparison — preparing the ground for future empirical research including co-design with persons with disabilities, usability studies, and pilot studies at destinations.

Methodological Note. This tool does not constitute exhaustive empirical validation, but rather a functional translation of the conceptual framework oriented toward exploring its internal coherence, implementation potential, and the hypotheses that could be tested in future research (Snyder, 2019). The research adopts an exploratory and propositional methodology based on a critical literature review spanning digital tourism, accessibility, disability studies, and algorithmic ethics.

Guiding Principles

Rights & Social Justice

Adopts the social model of disability: barriers are produced by social, normative, cultural, and technological structures, not inherent to the person.

Universal & Inclusive Design

All environments, services, and experiences must be usable by all persons without additional adaptations.

Personalized AI Technologies

Generative AI, computer vision, NLP, and adaptive recommendations adjusted to each user's functional needs and context.

Critical & Ethical Evaluation

Ethical oversight and continuous evaluation to minimize algorithmic bias, ensure transparency, and prevent inequality reproduction.

Conceptual Model: TAP-IAP

The TAP-IAP model is composed of four interrelated blocks that articulate critical disability theory, technological development, and tourism practice. Each block represents an intervention level and is explicitly linked to the research propositions (PI1–PI5), enabling visualization of the internal coherence of the proposed conceptual framework.

Model Components

PI2, PI4

Inclusive Technology Modules

Intelligent assistants, computer vision, barrier recognition, and cognitive adaptations represent AI technologies oriented toward identifying obstacles in the tourism environment and dynamically adapting the experience to diverse functional needs.

PI3, PI4

Ethical Algorithmic Personalization

Adaptive recommendation systems, decision explainability mechanisms, and bias minimization strategies introduce a normative and critical dimension, analyzing social, ethical, and algorithmic risks when AI interprets visitor needs.

PI1, PI2

Tourism Experience Mediators

Perceived autonomy, self-confidence, cognitive barrier reduction, and sense of dignity act as linking elements between technological intervention and the user's subjective experience in functional, emotional, and symbolic dimensions.

PI1, PI3, PI4

Expected Outcomes (Hypothetical)

Full participation, greater satisfaction, reduction of accessibility gaps, and effective inclusion reflect the anticipated effects of the articulation between technology, experiential mediation, and ethical principles.

TAP-IAP Integrative Level (PI5)

The articulation of all four blocks constitutes the response to PI5: How can a conceptual model critically integrate accessibility frameworks with current technological advances? The model offers an interpretive framework that coherently and socially responsibly guides the design of inclusive tourism experiences.

Research Propositions

PI1

How do physical, cognitive, communicative, and attitudinal barriers influence the tourism experience of persons with disabilities, in both its functional and emotional-symbolic dimensions?

PI2

How can personalized AI contribute to expanding the tourism autonomy of persons with disabilities, addressing their diverse functional needs?

PI3

What social, ethical, and algorithmic risks emerge when AI technologies intervene in interpreting visitors' needs, preferences, and capabilities?

PI4

What principles of universal accessibility and technological justice should guide the design of AI-based tourism systems to prevent new inequalities?

PI5

How can a conceptual model critically integrate accessibility frameworks with current technological advances?

Methodological Significance. This operationalization table bridges the gap between the TAP-IAP theoretical model and empirical research by specifying how each abstract construct can be observed, measured, and analyzed. It supports: (1) hypothesis formulation for future empirical studies, (2) design criteria for co-creation processes with persons with disabilities, (3) evaluation frameworks for public policy on accessible smart tourism, and (4) benchmarking standards for AI-driven tourism systems.

Decision-Support & Ex Ante Evaluation Tool

This interactive tool operationalizes the TAP-IAP model flow: (a) functional profiling, (b) personalization engine with traceability, (c) algorithmic accessibility module, and (d) indicator dashboard with scenario comparison. Navigate the tabs below to explore each module.

Module A: Functional Profiling

Captures user preferences, accessibility needs, and contextual variables to build a comprehensive functional profile. This module operationalizes the TAP-IAP principle that personalization must begin from a rights-based understanding of diverse functional needs rather than deficit-based categorization.

Functional Profile

Select all applicable functional characteristics. Multiple selections are supported to reflect intersectional needs.

Support Needs

Indicate the level and type of support typically required during travel.

Tourism Preferences

Indicate preferred tourism activities and interests.

Travel Context

Contextual variables that affect accessibility requirements.

Information & Interface Preferences

How information should be presented and at what complexity level.

Privacy & Data Consent

Control how your functional profile data is used. Aligned with GDPR and ethical AI principles.

Module B: Personalization Engine with Traceability

Generates personalized tourism recommendations with full algorithmic traceability — explaining why each recommendation is made and what data informs it. This module operationalizes the TAP-IAP components of adaptive recommendation systems and decision explainability.
